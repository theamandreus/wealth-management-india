## SIP Calculator Formula

**Monthly SIP needed** = Target Amount × [r / ((1+r)^n - 1)]

Where:
- r = monthly return rate (annual/12)
- n = number of months

## Goal-by-Goal Planning Playbook

### Retirement (FIRE or Traditional)

**First principles:** How much do you need?
1. Current annual expenses: ₹_____
2. Inflate at 6% to retirement age: ₹_____ (future annual need)
3. Multiply by 33 (for 3% withdrawal rate): ₹_____ = **target corpus**
4. Subtract: EPF + PPF + NPS lump sum + pension + rental income
5. Gap = what MF portfolio must fill

**SIP strategy:**
- Use Step-Up SIP (increase 10% annually with salary hike)
- Map to Aggressive or Moderate portfolio from model-portfolios.md
- Shift to Bucket Strategy (model-portfolios.md) 5 years before retirement

**Related skills:** india-financial-plan (full plan), india-nps-planner (NPS piece), india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 5 (SWP design)

### Child Education

**Key decision:** India vs Abroad

| Factor | India | Abroad |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Cost range | ₹15L-40L | ₹40L-1.5Cr |
| Inflation | 10% education | 10% + currency depreciation (3-4% extra) |
| Funding | SIP in equity MF | SIP in equity + international MF |
| Backup plan | Education loan (80E deduction) | Education loan + scholarship |
| Sukanya Samriddhi | Yes (if daughter <10) | Yes (use for initial funding) |

**Timeline-based strategy:**
- 15+ years to goal → 80% equity (mid + large cap)
- 10-15 years → 70% equity, start reducing at 10 years
- 5-10 years → 50% equity, 50% debt
- <5 years → Shift entirely to debt/liquid
- <2 years → 100% liquid/short duration

### House Down Payment

**Typical need:** 20% of property value + registration + furnishing

For a ₹1Cr property:
- Down payment: ₹20L
- Registration + stamp duty: ₹7-8L (varies by state, Karnataka ~5.6% + 1%)
- Furnishing: ₹5-10L
- **Total needed: ₹32-38L**

**If 5+ years away:** 50% Balanced Advantage + 50% Short Duration Debt
**If 3-5 years away:** 30% Conservative Hybrid + 70% Short Duration
**If <3 years:** 100% Short Duration Debt + Liquid Fund

**Related skill:** india-home-loan-planner (tax benefits once you buy)

---

### Emergency Fund

**How much:** 6 months expenses (conservative) to 12 months (if single income, freelancer, or volatile industry)

**Where:**
- 2 months in savings account (instant access)
- 4-10 months in Liquid Fund (T+1 redemption, better returns than savings)
- Do NOT put emergency fund in equity, FD with penalty, or locked instruments

**Build first:** This comes before ANY equity investment. Non-negotiable.

### FIRE (Financial Independence, Retire Early)

**India FIRE number:** 33x annual expenses (3% withdrawal rate, not 4%)

| Current Annual Expenses | FIRE Corpus (33x) | Monthly SIP for 15 years (at 12%) |
|------------------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| ₹6L (₹50K/month) | ₹2Cr | ₹42,000 |
| ₹12L (₹1L/month) | ₹4Cr | ₹84,000 |
| ₹18L (₹1.5L/month) | ₹6Cr | ₹1,26,000 |
| ₹24L (₹2L/month) | ₹8Cr | ₹1,68,000 |

**India-specific FIRE considerations:**
- Healthcare: No Medicare. Budget ₹50L-1Cr for lifetime health costs at 10% inflation
- Parents: May need to support parents — include in expenses
- Inflation: 6%, not 2.5% — your corpus erodes faster
- No Social Security: EPF pension (EPS) is negligible (₹1-9K/month)
- Health insurance: Must maintain personal policy (no employer cover after FIRE)
