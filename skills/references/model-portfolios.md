Ready-to-use portfolio templates for Indian investors. Match to risk profile from india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 1.

**Rule**: Never more than 8 funds. Each fund should have a clear, non-overlapping role.

## First-Time Investor Starter Portfolios

### "I have ₹5K-10K/month and zero market experience"
- **Month 1-3**: Build emergency fund (6 months expenses) in Liquid Fund
- **Month 4+**: Start 1 SIP — Nifty 50 Index Fund (₹5K) or Flexi Cap (₹10K)
- **After 6 months**: Add Mid Cap SIP if comfortable with equity volatility
- **Don't**: Start with small cap, thematic, or NFOs

### "I have a ₹5L lump sum and want to start investing"
- Park ₹5L in Liquid Fund
- Start weekly STP over 6 months into Flexi Cap or Nifty 50 Index
- Simultaneously start monthly SIP from salary
- See india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 5 for STP duration table

### "I just want to save tax under 80C"
- ELSS mutual fund — 3-year lock-in, equity returns
- Start SIP in April (not March rush)
- See india-tax-optimizer for full 80C priority matrix

## Short-Term Goal Portfolios (1-5 years)

| Goal Horizon | Asset Mix | Vehicles |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| <1 year | 100% debt | Liquid Fund, Overnight Fund, or Arbitrage Fund |
| 1-2 years | 80% debt + 20% equity | Short Duration MF + Arbitrage Fund |
| 2-3 years | 70% debt + 30% equity | Short Duration + Conservative Hybrid |
| 3-5 years | 50% debt + 50% equity | Balanced Advantage Fund + Short Duration |
| 5+ years | Follow risk-profile portfolio above | Equity-dominant as per risk profile |

**Critical rule**: Money needed within 3 years should NEVER be majority in equity. A -30% crash right before your goal date destroys the plan.

## Retirement Income Portfolio (SWP Design)

For someone in retirement drawing regular income:

| Bucket | Allocation | Vehicle | Withdrawal Source |
|--------|-----------|---------|------------------|
| Bucket 1 (0-2 years) | 20% | Liquid + Short Duration Debt | SWP from here |
| Bucket 2 (2-5 years) | 30% | Conservative Hybrid / BAF | Refills Bucket 1 annually |
| Bucket 3 (5+ years) | 40% | Flexi Cap + Large Cap | Refills Bucket 2 when markets are up |
| Bucket 4 (Permanent) | 10% | PPF (if active) + SGB | Safety net + inflation hedge |

**SWP rate**: 3% of total corpus per year (₹1Cr = ₹25,000/month). Increase 6% annually for inflation.

See india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 5 for detailed SWP vs FD comparison.

## Shariah-Compliant Model Portfolios

For investors who require 100% Shariah-compliant investments. See india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 11 for full Shariah fund universe and screening criteria.

**Key constraint**: No debt MFs, no FDs, no interest-bearing instruments. Portfolio will be equity-heavy by necessity.

### Shariah Starter (₹3K-5K/month SIP)

| Allocation | Vehicle | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| 100% | Tata Ethical Fund (Direct) | Oldest ethical fund (since 1996), TASIS certified, decent AUM, single fund simplicity |

**Alternative**: Nifty 50 Shariah Index Fund (if available in Direct) for passive approach.
At ₹3-5K, one fund is optimal. Don't split further.

### Shariah Moderate (₹10K-25K/month SIP)

| Allocation | Vehicle | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| 50% | Tata Ethical Fund (Direct) | Active management, broad ethical equity |
| 30% | Nifty 500 Shariah Index Fund/ETF | Broader universe, passive, low cost |
| 20% | Gold ETF or SGB* | Non-equity diversification, inflation hedge |

*SGB pays 2.5% interest — some scholars consider this non-compliant. Consult your scholar. Gold ETF is the alternative (no interest, but no tax-free maturity benefit).

### Shariah Aggressive (₹25K+/month SIP)

| Allocation | Vehicle | Why |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| 40% | Tata Ethical Fund (Direct) | Core active holding |
| 25% | Nifty 50 Shariah Index Fund | Large-cap passive |
| 15% | Nifty 500 Shariah Index Fund | Mid/small cap exposure via broader index |
| 10% | Quantum Ethical Fund | Second active manager, different screening |
| 10% | Gold ETF | Non-equity anchor |

### Shariah Portfolio Limitations (set expectations)

| Factor | Conventional Portfolio | Shariah Portfolio |
|--------|----------------------|------------------|
| Expected CAGR (long-term) | 12-14% | 10-12% |
| Sector diversification | Full (incl. banking 35%) | Partial (no banking/NBFC/insurance) |
| Debt allocation options | Full (debt MF, FD, PPF) | Very limited (gold only) |
| Volatility | Lower (equity + debt mix) | Higher (mostly equity, no debt buffer) |
| Number of fund options | 1000+ | 4-5 |

**Mitigation for sector concentration**: Accept it. The trade-off is values-alignment. Don't try to "fix" the banking gap with non-compliant funds — that defeats the purpose. The IT/pharma/FMCG tilt has historically been quality-biased, which partially compensates.

## Notes

- All return assumptions are nominal (before inflation). Real returns = nominal - 6% inflation.
- "Direct" means Direct Plan MF (no commission). Always prefer Direct over Regular.
- Rebalance when any asset class drifts >10% from target. See india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 6.
- Tax harvesting: Book ₹1.25L LTCG annually to use exemption. See india-tax-optimizer Step 5.
- These are templates. Actual allocation should be personalized based on risk profiling (india-mutual-fund-advisor Step 1).
