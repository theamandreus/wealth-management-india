# India Tax Rates — FY 2025-26 / FY 2026-27

**Last updated:** February 2026
**Source:** Income Tax Act (replaced by Income Tax Act, 2025 effective 1 April 2026), Union Budget 2025 & 2026

> **Important:** The Income Tax Act, 2025 replaces the 1961 Act from 1 April 2026 with simplified language and rationalised compliance. Slab rates and deduction limits remain the same for FY 2026-27.

---

## New Tax Regime (Default)

The New Regime is the default option from FY 2023-24 onwards. Taxpayer must actively opt for Old Regime if preferred.

| Taxable Income (₹) | Tax Rate |
|-------------------|---------|
| Up to 2,50,000 | Nil |
| 2,50,001 – 4,00,000 | Nil |
| 4,00,001 – 5,00,000 | 5% |
| 5,00,001 – 8,00,000 | 5% |
| 8,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 10% |
| 10,00,001 – 12,00,000 | 10% |
| 12,00,001 – 16,00,000 | 15% |
| 16,00,001 – 20,00,000 | 20% |
| 20,00,001 – 24,00,000 | 25% |
| Above 24,00,000 | 30% |

**Basic exemption:** ₹4,00,000
**Standard deduction (salaried):** ₹75,000
**Section 87A rebate:** ₹60,000 (income up to ₹12L = zero tax)
**Effective tax-free income (salaried):** ₹12,75,000 (₹12L + ₹75K standard deduction)
**Health & Education Cess:** 4% on tax amount

**Deductions allowed in New Regime:**
- ₹75,000 standard deduction (salaried)
- Employer's NPS contribution under 80CCD(2) — up to 10% of Basic + DA
- Family pension deduction — ₹25,000 or 1/3rd of pension (lower)
- NO 80C, 80D, HRA, Section 24, 80CCD(1B), 80E, 80G, 80TTA

## Old Tax Regime (Optional)

Must be actively opted for. Available to salaried (can switch each year) and self-employed (restrictions on switching back).

| Taxable Income (₹) | Tax Rate (Below 60) | Tax Rate (60-80 — Senior) | Tax Rate (80+ — Super Senior) |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|------------------------------|
| Up to 2,50,000 | Nil | Nil (up to ₹3L) | Nil (up to ₹5L) |
| 2,50,001 – 5,00,000 | 5% | 5% (₹3L-5L) | Nil |
| 5,00,001 – 10,00,000 | 20% | 20% | 20% |
| Above 10,00,000 | 30% | 30% | 30% |

**Standard deduction:** ₹50,000
**All deductions available:** 80C, 80D, 80CCD(1B), 80E, 80G, 80TTA/TTB, Section 24(b), HRA

## Old vs New Regime Decision Rule

**Breakeven point:** If your total deductions under Old Regime exceed approximately ₹4-4.5L (varies by income level), Old Regime is likely better.

**Simplified rule of thumb:**
- Income ₹7-12L → New Regime almost always wins (87A rebate makes it tax-free)
- Income ₹12-20L → Calculate both; Old wins only if heavy deductions (home loan + 80C + 80D + HRA)
- Income >₹20L → Old Regime often wins for salaried with home loan + HRA + full 80C/80D
- Self-employed with no HRA → New Regime usually wins

## Capital Gains Tax (Post Finance Act 2024)

| Asset | STCG Period | STCG Rate | LTCG Period | LTCG Rate | Exemption |
|-------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Listed equity shares | ≤12 months | 20% | >12 months | 12.5% | ₹1.25L/year |
| Equity MF (≥65% equity) | ≤12 months | 20% | >12 months | 12.5% | ₹1.25L/year |
| Debt MF | Any period | Slab rate | Any period | Slab rate | None (post Apr 2023) |
| Hybrid MF (≥65% equity) | ≤12 months | 20% | >12 months | 12.5% | ₹1.25L/year |
| Hybrid MF (<65% equity) | Any period | Slab rate | Any period | Slab rate | None |
| Gold / Gold ETF / SGB | ≤24 months | Slab rate | >24 months | 12.5% | None |
| SGB (held to maturity) | N/A | N/A | 8 years | **TAX FREE** | Full exemption |
| Real estate | ≤24 months | Slab rate | >24 months | 12.5% | Sec 54/54F reinvestment |
| Unlisted shares | ≤24 months | Slab rate | >24 months | 12.5% | None |

**India advantage — No wash sale rules:**
- Sell to book loss → buy back same stock/MF immediately
- Loss is still valid for offset
- Use this aggressively for tax harvesting

**LTCG harvesting strategy:** Book ₹1.25L LTCG every year (even if profitable) to use annual exemption. Rebuy immediately.

## Other Key Tax Updates (2025-2026)

| Change | Detail | Impact |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Income Tax Act 2025 | Replaces 1961 Act from 1 April 2026 | Simplified language, same rates. Rationalised compliance. |
| Senior citizen TDS limit | Doubled to ₹1,00,000 | Less TDS deducted on FD/savings interest for seniors |
| Revised return deadline | Extended to 31 March (from 31 December) | More time to file revised ITR, subject to nominal fee |
| TCS on overseas tour/LRS | Standardised at flat 2% for education/medical | Reduced from previous higher rates |
| STT increase | Equity futures: 0.05%, Options: 0.15% | Higher cost for F&O traders |
| Surcharge | Max 25% on income >₹2Cr (Old), 25% on >₹2Cr (New) | Effective max rate ~39% (Old) |
