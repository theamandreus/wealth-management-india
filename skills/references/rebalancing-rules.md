# Rebalancing Rules & Portfolio Health (India)

When, why, and how to rebalance — with safety rules to protect the investor.

## Who Needs Rebalancing?

| Investor Type | Needs Rebalancing? | Why |
|--------------|-------------------|-----|
| SIP investor with 2-3 funds, 5+ year horizon | Rarely | SIP auto-averages. Check annually. |
| Portfolio with 5+ funds across categories | Yes, half-yearly | Drift accumulates across many funds |
| Investor with lump sum invested 2+ years ago | Yes, check now | Bull/bear markets shift allocation significantly |
| Retiree on SWP | Yes, quarterly | Bucket refills needed to maintain income |
| Goal within 3 years of target date | Yes, critical | Must shift from equity to debt/liquid |
| Someone who hasn't reviewed portfolio in 2+ years | Yes, urgently | Likely significant drift + underperforming funds |

## Rebalancing Triggers

| Trigger | What Happened | Action |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Equity drifted >10% above target | Bull market inflated equity portion | Redirect SIPs to debt OR sell equity + buy debt |
| Equity drifted >10% below target | Bear market compressed equity | STP from debt to equity OR increase equity SIP |
| Single fund >25% of total portfolio | One fund outperformed disproportionately | Trim excess, add to underweight fund |
| Goal within 3 years | Approaching target date | STP entire goal corpus from equity → short duration debt/liquid |
| Single stock/direct equity >15% of portfolio | Concentration risk | Trim gradually, diversify into MF |
| Tax harvesting window (Jan-Mar) | Financial year ending | Book LTCG up to ₹1.25L, rebuy immediately (no wash sale in India) |
| Life event | Job change, child born, inheritance | Review full allocation against new reality |

## How to Rebalance (Methods — Ranked by Tax Efficiency)

| Method | Tax Impact | Best When |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| **1. Redirect future SIPs** | Zero tax | Mild drift (5-10%). Redirect new SIPs to underweight asset. Slowest but cheapest. |
| **2. STP from overweight to underweight** | Minimal (liquid fund gains are small) | Moderate drift (10-15%). Set up STP within same AMC. |
| **3. New lump sum into underweight** | Zero tax | You have bonus/windfall. Deploy into underweight asset. |
| **4. Sell overweight + buy underweight** | LTCG/STCG applies | Severe drift (>15%). Only if units are >12 months (LTCG). Use ₹1.25L exemption. |

## Safety Rules (Non-Negotiable)

These rules PROTECT the investor from common rebalancing mistakes:

1. **NEVER sell equity during a >20% market crash to "cut losses"** — This is the #1 wealth destroyer. Crashes are when equity is cheapest. Hold or add.

2. **NEVER rebalance into something riskier than the investor's risk profile** — If they're Moderate, don't push to 90% equity even if "the market looks good."

3. **Always check tax impact BEFORE any sell action** — Is the gain LTCG or STCG? How much of the ₹1.25L annual LTCG exemption is left? Will this push them into a higher tax bracket?

4. **Prefer SIP/STP redirection over selling** — Selling creates tax events. Redirecting future money is free.

5. **If in doubt, do nothing** — Inaction in volatile markets is usually the right call. The cost of wrong rebalancing > cost of being slightly off-target.

6. **Never rebalance based on market predictions** — "I think the market will crash, let me move to debt" is not rebalancing. It's market timing. Don't.

7. **Rebalance on schedule, not on emotion** — Set calendar reminders (half-yearly or annually). Don't rebalance because you "feel" the market is too high or too low.

## Portfolio Health Score — 6 Metrics

Use this scorecard to assess any Indian MF portfolio:

| # | Metric | Healthy (Green) | Warning (Yellow) | Unhealthy (Red) | How to Check |
|---|--------|----------------|-----------------|-----------------|-------------|
| 1 | Equity:Debt ratio vs target | Within ±5% | ±5-10% drift | >10% drift | Sum all equity fund values vs debt fund values |
| 2 | Fund overlap | <30% across all funds | 30-50% | >50% | Use Value Research or Morningstar overlap tool |
| 3 | Number of funds | 4-8 | 8-12 | >12 | Count active SIPs + lump sum holdings |
| 4 | Direct vs Regular plans | 100% Direct | Some Regular | Majority Regular | Check plan type on CAS statement |
| 5 | Underperforming funds | 0 | 1 fund below category average for 2+ years | Multiple funds | Compare rolling returns to category on Value Research |
| 6 | Goal coverage | All goals have mapped SIPs | Some goals unmapped | Major goals unfunded | List goals → check if each has a dedicated SIP |

**Scoring:** Green = 2 points, Yellow = 1 point, Red = 0 points. Total out of 12.
- 10-12: Healthy portfolio, minor tweaks only
- 7-9: Needs attention on specific metrics
- <7: Full portfolio restructuring recommended

## Hidden Bond/Debt Exposure Detection

Many "equity" funds carry significant debt/bond allocation that investors don't realize:

### Where Hidden Bonds Live

| Fund Category | Expected Equity % | Common Hidden Debt % | Risk |
|--------------|-------------------|---------------------|------|
| Balanced Advantage Fund (BAF) | 65-80% (gross) | 20-50% net equity after hedging | Returns lag pure equity in bull markets. Investors expect equity returns but get hybrid returns. |
| Conservative Hybrid | 10-25% equity | 75-90% debt | Marketed as "safe equity" but it's essentially a debt fund with equity taxation pre-2023 |
| Aggressive Hybrid | 65-80% equity | 20-35% debt | Acceptable if investor knows. Problem when they think it's pure equity. |
| Multi-Asset Allocation | Varies wildly | 20-40% debt + 10-20% gold | Useful for conservative but misleading if counted as "equity" allocation |
| Equity Savings Fund | 15-40% net equity | 60-85% debt + arbitrage | Essentially a debt product with equity label |
| Arbitrage Fund | ~0% net equity | 100% (equity-debt arbitrage) | Pure debt-like returns with equity taxation |
| Dynamic Asset Allocation | 30-80% (changes) | Fund manager decides | Allocation changes quarterly — you don't control it |

### How to Detect Hidden Bond Exposure

1. **Read the monthly factsheet** — Every fund publishes asset allocation. Check "Net Equity" not "Gross Equity." BAFs hedge equity with derivatives, so gross equity is misleading.

2. **Check "Portfolio Equity %" on Value Research / Morningstar** — This shows actual equity exposure, not category label.

3. **Calculate your TRUE portfolio equity %:**
   - List each fund's current value
   - Multiply by that fund's actual equity % (from factsheet)
   - Sum = your real equity exposure
   - Compare to your target allocation

4. **Warning signs of over-exposure to hidden debt:**
   - Portfolio returns consistently 3-4% below Nifty in bull years → you have more debt than you think
   - You have 5 "equity" funds but your XIRR is 8% over 5 years → hidden debt drag
   - Multiple BAFs + aggressive hybrids counted as "equity" → actual equity may be 40% when you think it's 70%

### When Hidden Bonds HURT the Portfolio

| Situation | Problem | Fix |
|-----------|---------|-----|
| Young investor (25-35) with 20-year horizon has 3 BAFs as "equity" | Actual equity exposure ~45% instead of 70%. Losing 15+ years of equity compounding. | Replace BAFs with pure equity (index + active). Keep 1 BAF max for stability. |
| Multiple "equity savings" funds counted as equity | Real equity is 20-30%. Massively under-allocated to equity. | Replace with actual equity MFs. Use equity savings only for <3 year goals. |
| Retiree has all corpus in flexi cap (100% equity) | No debt buffer. A -30% crash wipes 30% of retirement income. | Move to bucket strategy (model-portfolios.md) with 20% liquid + 30% hybrid + 40% equity. |
| 4 hybrid funds overlapping | Paying 4 expense ratios for essentially similar allocation. Category overlap >60%. | Consolidate to 1 BAF + pure equity funds + pure debt funds. |

### When Hidden Bonds Are FINE

- Retiree wanting simple portfolio: 1 BAF for SWP is perfectly valid
- First-time investor who can't stomach volatility: BAF as stepping stone
- Short-term goal (3-5 years): Conservative hybrid is appropriate
- Tax optimization: Arbitrage fund for 1+ year parking (equity LTCG at 12.5% beats debt fund at slab rate)

### Portfolio Audit Quick Check

Run this check on any portfolio:

```
For each fund:
  1. What SEBI category is it? (Check on AMFI website)
  2. What is the ACTUAL equity allocation? (Check latest factsheet)
  3. Is this fund serving a clear, non-overlapping role?
  4. Does the investor KNOW the actual asset allocation?

Red flags:
  - 3+ hybrid funds → consolidate
  - Any fund where investor thinks equity % is different from actual → educate
  - Total portfolio actual equity deviates >10% from target → rebalance
  - More than 50% overlap between any two funds → eliminate one
```
